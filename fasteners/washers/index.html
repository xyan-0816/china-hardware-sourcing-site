Flat Washers, Spring Washers and Industrial Washers

Washers should be specified by washer type, standard, inner diameter, outer diameter, thickness, material, hardness where required, finish, quantity, packing, inspection documents, and matching bolt or nut requirements before RFQ review. This page supports manual sourcing communication for flat washers, spring washers, hardened washers, structural washers, fender washers, square washers, lock washers, and plain washers.

Direct Answer for Buyers

For a washer RFQ, buyers should first confirm whether the item follows DIN 125, ISO 7089, ISO 7090, DIN 127, DIN 9021, DIN 6796, ASTM F436, ASTM F844, or a buyer drawing. Flat washers, spring washers, fender washers, hardened washers, structural washers, square washers, lock washers, and plain washers should be listed as separate SKUs when dimensions, hardness, material, or finish differ.

Washers are often purchased with matching bolts, nuts, studs, threaded rods, cartons, pallets, coating requirements, and inspection documents. Prices, MOQ, delivery lead time, packing details, and documentation are confirmed after manual RFQ review.

Final specifications should be confirmed according to the buyer's drawing, BOM, project standard, or RFQ requirements.

Common Procurement Mistakes

  • Quoting washers only by bolt diameter without ID, OD, and thickness.
  • Mixing flat washers and spring washers in one unclear line item.
  • Ignoring washer hardness for high-strength or structural assemblies.
  • Choosing galvanized washers without checking matching bolt and nut finish.
  • Underestimating carton weight for bulk washer packing.
  • Using a photo instead of a standard, drawing, or BOM.
  • Assuming large OD washers and normal flat washers are interchangeable.

Product-Level FAQ

Short answers for buyers preparing technical washer RFQs.

What is the difference between flat washers and spring washers?

Flat washers mainly help distribute load and protect contact surfaces, while spring washers are selected for applications where a buyer wants a locking or vibration-resistance function. The final washer type should follow the drawing, BOM, project standard, or RFQ requirements.

How should buyers specify washer size in an RFQ?

Buyers should specify washer type, standard, inner diameter, outer diameter, thickness, material, hardness where required, finish, quantity, packing, and matching bolt or nut size.

When are hardened washers required?

Hardened washers may be required for high-strength bolt assemblies, structural connections, or applications where the project standard calls for washer hardness control. Buyers should confirm hardness, standard reference, coating, and mating bolt grade before RFQ review.

Can washers be ordered together with bolts and nuts?

Yes. Washers can be reviewed together with bolts, nuts, studs, and threaded rods when each SKU includes standard, size, material, finish, quantity, packing, and document requirements.

What surface finish should be used for outdoor washer applications?

Outdoor washer applications often require hot-dip galvanized, zinc flake, stainless steel, or another buyer-specified corrosion-resistant finish. The finish should be reviewed with the mating bolt and nut finish, coating thickness, and project requirement.

What affects the lead time for a mixed washer order from China?

Lead time depends on washer type, standard, material, finish, quantity, packing, and inspection document requirements. Final lead time is confirmed after RFQ review.
