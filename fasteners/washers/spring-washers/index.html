Spring Washers, Split Lock Washers and Disc Springs

Spring washers from China are commonly sourced for bolted assemblies where elastic tension, locking action or load-deflection performance is required. Dimensions and Form Considerations

Spring washer dimensions and form affect elastic performance, load-deflection characteristics and assembly fit. The following parameters should be reviewed together with the applicable standard and buyer requirements.

  • Nominal bolt size
  • Inside diameter
  • Outside diameter
  • Thickness or wire diameter
  • Form (split, wave, conical, disc, single-coil)
  • Free height
  • Compressed height
  • Deflection range
  • Flatness where applicable
  • Concentricity
  • Dimensional tolerance
  • Thickness or wire diameter tolerance
  • Surface condition
  • Stamping marks
  • Edge and burr condition
  • Custom dimensions
  • Drawing-controlled products

Key considerations:

  1. The nominal bolt size does not equal the washer's actual inside diameter.
  2. The same inside diameter may correspond to different forms, outside diameters and thicknesses.
  3. Split lock washers, wave washers and disc springs have different form characteristics and cannot be confirmed by name alone.
  4. Wire diameter affects elastic force and must be confirmed per the applicable standard or drawing.
  5. Where precise load-deflection performance is required, a complete drawing or performance specification should be provided.
  6. Spring washer dimensions must not be confirmed from weight or a product photo alone.

Common Procurement Mistakes

The following mistakes are commonly encountered in spring washer procurement. Reviewing them before submitting an RFQ can reduce specification errors and quotation delays.

  1. Calling every elastic washer a "spring washer" without confirming the type, form or standard.
  2. Quoting only the nominal bolt size without confirming the actual inside diameter.
  3. Assuming the nominal bolt size equals the washer's actual inside diameter.
  4. Forgetting to specify the outside diameter.
  5. Forgetting to specify the thickness or wire diameter.
  6. Treating split lock washers and wave washers as interchangeable.
  7. Treating conical spring washers and disc springs as standard split lock washers.
  8. Treating serrated washers and tooth lock washers as interchangeable with split lock washers.
  9. Using a plain flat washer where a spring washer is required, or vice versa.
  10. Assuming DIN and ASME references are automatically identical.
  11. Assuming spring washers guarantee vibration resistance in all applications.
  12. Identifying washer hardness or elastic performance only by colour or appearance.
  13. Selecting a coating without checking compatibility with mating fasteners.
  14. Assuming black oxide provides high outdoor corrosion resistance.
  15. Ignoring deflection or free height requirements.
  16. Using multiple spring washers to compensate for an incorrect bolt length without design approval.
  17. Mixing different forms or dimensions in one unclear RFQ line.
  18. Approving production only from a representative image.
  19. Requesting hardness or elastic tests only after production.
  20. Failing to define export packing and label requirements.

Product-Level FAQ

What is the difference between a split lock washer and a spring washer?

A split lock washer is a specific type of spring washer with a helical split ring that creates a locking action when compressed. The term spring washer is broader and may include wave washers, conical spring washers, single-coil spring washers and disc springs. Buyers should confirm the specific washer type, standard and dimensions.

What information should buyers provide for a spring washer RFQ?

Buyers should provide washer type, applicable standard or drawing, metric or inch series, nominal bolt size, inside diameter, outside diameter, thickness or wire diameter, form, material, hardness or elastic requirement, surface finish, matching bolt and nut standard, application, quantity by SKU, packing, inspection documents and destination.

Can a split lock washer be substituted with a wave washer?

No. A split lock washer and a wave washer use different elastic principles and have different load-deflection characteristics. They are not automatically interchangeable. Buyers should confirm the washer type per the applicable standard or project specification.

What is the difference between a conical spring washer and a disc spring / Belleville washer?

A conical spring washer is a conically shaped elastic washer. A disc spring or Belleville washer is also conically shaped but is designed for specific load-deflection characteristics per applicable standards. They may appear similar but have different design logic, dimensional requirements and performance characteristics. Buyers should confirm the specific product type, standard and application requirements.

Can a disc spring be treated as a standard split lock washer?

No. Disc springs and Belleville washers have a different load and deflection design logic from split lock washers. They are not interchangeable. Disc springs are designed for specific load-deflection performance and must be confirmed per the applicable standard and application requirements.

Are DIN 127 and ASME B18.21.1 spring washers automatically identical?

No. DIN 127 and ASME B18.21.1 are different standards from different standardisation systems. They may have differences in dimensions, material requirements and testing methods. Buyers should confirm the applicable standard, version and requirements for their specific product.

What is the difference between a serrated washer and a split lock washer?

A serrated washer has serrations or teeth on the bearing surface that mechanically engage the mating surface to resist loosening. A split lock washer uses a helical split ring that creates elastic tension when compressed. They use different locking principles and are not automatically interchangeable.

Can a tooth lock washer be substituted for a split lock washer?

No. A tooth lock washer uses multiple teeth that bite into the mating surface. A split lock washer uses a helical split ring. They use different locking principles and may not be interchangeable without reviewing the application requirements.

Can a plain flat washer be used instead of a spring washer?

No. A plain flat washer is a flat, non-elastic washer used for load distribution. A spring washer is designed to provide elastic tension or locking action. They serve different functions and are not interchangeable.

Does a spring washer guarantee vibration resistance?

No. Spring washer vibration resistance depends on the product form, material, hardness, compression, mating fastener, surface condition and actual assembly conditions. No spring washer type can be guaranteed to prevent loosening in all applications. Assembly testing and engineering verification are required.

What materials are commonly used for spring washers?

Common materials include spring steel, stainless steel A2 and A4, and other buyer-specified materials. Material selection depends on the application, elastic requirement, corrosion resistance and temperature. Buyers should confirm the specific material and hardness per the applicable standard or drawing.

Why must spring washer hardness be confirmed?

Hardness affects the elastic performance, load-deflection characteristics and locking behaviour of the spring washer. A washer with incorrect hardness may not provide the intended elastic tension or may fail under load. Hardness must be confirmed per the applicable standard and application requirements.

Can different spring washer types be included in one mixed order?

Yes. Mixed orders can be reviewed manually when washer type, standard, form, dimensions, material, hardness, finish, quantity, packing and inspection requirements are clear for each SKU.

What inspection documents can be discussed?

Depending on the item and buyer requirements, documents may include dimensional reports, material test certificates, hardness test reports, coating thickness reports, packing lists and pre-shipment photos.

What affects the lead time for a mixed spring washer order?

Lead time depends on material availability, washer type, standard, dimensions, hardness requirement, heat treatment if applicable, coating, quantity, packing requirements and inspection requirements. Final lead time is confirmed after RFQ review.

Can single-coil and heavy-duty spring washers be treated as interchangeable?

No. Single-coil spring washers and heavy-duty spring washers have different wire diameters, load characteristics and applications. They are not automatically interchangeable. Buyers should confirm the specific type, dimensions and application requirements.
