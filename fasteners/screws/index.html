Machine Screws, Self-Tapping Screws and Self-Drilling Screws

Screws should be specified by screw type, head type, drive type, thread type, point type, diameter, length, material, finish, substrate, quantity, packing, and inspection document requirements before RFQ review. This page supports manual sourcing communication for machine screws, self-tapping screws, self-drilling screws, sheet metal screws, socket head screws, construction screws, and specialty screws made to drawing. Prices, MOQ, delivery lead time, packing details, and documentation are confirmed after manual RFQ review.

Direct Answer for Buyers

Buyers cannot identify a screw type only from a photo or a single word such as "screw". An RFQ should at least confirm screw type, head type, drive / recess type, thread type, point type, diameter, length, thread pitch where applicable, material, property class or hardness requirement where applicable, surface finish, substrate or application, quantity, packing, and inspection document requirements.

The following product categories must be listed as separate SKUs when they differ: machine screws, self-tapping screws, self-drilling screws, different head styles, different drive styles, different thread or point types, and different materials and finishes.

Prices, MOQ, packing, documentation, and lead time are confirmed after manual RFQ review.

Final specifications should be confirmed according to the buyer's drawing, BOM, project standard, sample, or RFQ requirements.

Product images are representative category references only. Final specifications should be confirmed by buyer drawings, standards, samples, or purchase lists.

Common Procurement Mistakes

  • Calling every product simply a "screw".
  • Identifying the product only from a photograph.
  • Confusing self-tapping screws with self-drilling screws.
  • Forgetting head type, drive type or point type.
  • Not confirming whether length includes the head.
  • Ignoring substrate material and thickness.
  • Ignoring pilot-hole requirements.
  • Assuming DIN and ISO references are automatically interchangeable.
  • Ignoring hardness or drilling performance requirements.
  • Selecting a finish without checking corrosion exposure.
  • Ignoring hydrogen embrittlement controls for hardened plated screws.
  • Mixing different screw types or finishes in one unclear RFQ line.
  • Not defining packing count, carton weight or labels.
  • Approving production only from a representative image.

Product-Level FAQ

Short answers for buyers preparing technical screw RFQs.

What is the difference between machine screws and self-tapping screws?

Machine screws are designed for use with pre-tapped holes or matching nuts and do not form their own threads. Self-tapping screws form or cut mating threads in the substrate material during installation. Buyers should confirm the application, substrate, and required standard before selecting the screw type.

What is the difference between self-tapping screws and self-drilling screws?

Self-tapping screws require a pre-drilled pilot hole to form or cut threads. Self-drilling screws have an integrated drill point that can drill through the substrate without a separate pilot hole. These two types should not be used interchangeably without confirming the application and substrate.

What information should buyers provide for a screw RFQ?

Buyers should provide screw type, head type, drive type, thread type, point type, diameter, length, thread pitch where applicable, material, property class or hardness, surface finish, substrate material and thickness, quantity, packing, and inspection document requirements.

How should screw length be measured?

Countersunk screw length is normally measured overall, while pan head or socket head screw length is measured from under the head. Buyers should confirm the required measurement method according to the applicable standard or drawing.

Can DIN and ISO screw standards be substituted?

Buyers should not treat DIN and ISO screw standards as automatically interchangeable. Dimensions, recess details, head geometry, thread form, and point type must be confirmed before substitution. Final specifications should follow the buyer's drawing, BOM, project standard, or RFQ requirements.

Which screw material and finish are suitable for outdoor applications?

Outdoor screw applications often require stainless steel, hot-dip galvanized, or zinc flake coating depending on substrate, corrosion exposure, and mating parts. Buyers should confirm the environment, coating performance, and project requirements before selection.

What affects the drilling performance of self-drilling screws?

Drilling performance depends on substrate material, substrate thickness, drill point design, screw hardness, drive system, and installation method. Buyers should confirm the specific drilling capacity with the supplier rather than assuming fixed values.

Why must buyers specify the head and drive type?

Head type and drive type determine tool fit, installation torque, appearance, and compatibility with the assembly design. Phillips, hex, Torx, slotted, square, and Pozi drives serve different applications and are not interchangeable without specification.

Can machine screws, tapping screws and drilling screws be included in one mixed order?

Yes. Mixed orders can be reviewed when each SKU includes screw type, head type, drive type, diameter, length, material, finish, quantity, packing, and document requirements. Different screw types should be listed as separate line items.

What inspection documents can be discussed for screw orders?

Depending on the screw type and buyer requirements, dimensional reports, material certificates, coating thickness reports, hardness tests, drilling performance tests, thread gauging reports, packing lists, and pre-shipment photos or inspection may be discussed before shipment.
