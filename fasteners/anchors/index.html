Wedge Anchors, Sleeve Anchors and Concrete Fastening Solutions

Concrete and structural anchors from China are commonly sourced for construction, infrastructure, and industrial fastening applications where secure fixing to concrete, masonry, or structural substrates is required. Common types include wedge anchors, sleeve anchors, drop-in anchors, anchor bolts, and expansion anchors. Buyers should confirm anchor type, diameter, overall length, embedment depth, thread size, material (carbon steel, stainless steel 304/316), and surface finish (zinc plating, hot-dip galvanizing, black oxide, or plain) before quotation. Standard references may include ASTM F1554, DIN 529, ICC-ES compliant designs, or project-specific drawings. We support sourcing communication for anchors with matching nuts and washers, mixed SKU orders, and Hebei / Yongnian fastener cluster sourcing where applicable. Prices, MOQ, delivery lead time, packing details, and documentation are confirmed after manual RFQ review. Product images are representative only; final specifications should be confirmed by buyer drawings, standards, samples, or purchase lists.

Common Procurement Mistakes

  • Calling every concrete fastener an "anchor bolt".
  • Confusing wedge anchors with sleeve anchors.
  • Identifying an anchor only from a photograph.
  • Not specifying the base material.
  • Assuming concrete, brick and hollow block use the same anchor data.
  • Ignoring cracked or uncracked concrete requirements.
  • Not confirming fixture thickness.
  • Ignoring embedment depth.
  • Ignoring hole diameter and hole depth.
  • Ignoring edge distance and anchor spacing.
  • Selecting products only by diameter.
  • Copying load data from another manufacturer or another anchor size.
  • Treating ASTM F1554 anchor rods as mechanical expansion anchors.
  • Claiming ETA or ICC-ES approval without confirming the exact product and report.
  • Ignoring installation torque.
  • Ignoring hole cleaning and installation procedure.
  • Mixing different diameters, lengths or finishes in one unclear RFQ line.
  • Approving structural anchors only from a representative image.

Product-Level FAQ

Short answers for buyers preparing technical anchor RFQs.

What is the difference between wedge anchors and sleeve anchors?

Wedge anchors expand by wedging a cone-shaped end against the base material, typically used in solid concrete. Sleeve anchors have a cylindrical sleeve that expands against the base material and may also be used in brick or block depending on the design. They are not interchangeable products.

Can wedge anchors be used in brick or hollow block?

Wedge anchors are designed primarily for solid concrete. Brick, hollow block and other masonry materials require different anchor designs and installation procedures. Base material must be confirmed before anchor selection.

What information should buyers provide for an anchor RFQ?

Buyers should provide anchor type, standard or drawing reference, base material, diameter, overall length, thread size, effective embedment depth, fixture thickness, hole diameter, finish, quantity, packing and inspection document requirements.

Why must buyers specify the base material?

Concrete, solid brick, hollow brick, block and stone are not the same base materials. Anchor performance and installation depend on the base material. The same anchor cannot automatically be used in different base materials.

What is effective embedment depth?

Effective embedment depth is the distance from the surface of the base material to the deepest point of the anchor engagement. It affects load capacity and must be specified rather than assumed from overall length.

Why are edge distance and anchor spacing important?

Edge distance and anchor spacing directly affect load capacity, concrete breakout resistance and installation safety. These parameters must be confirmed against the project design or applicable evaluation documentation.

Can anchor load data be selected only by diameter?

No. Load capacity depends on embedment depth, base material strength, edge distance, spacing, installation quality and the specific anchor design. Diameter alone is not sufficient to determine load performance.

What is the difference between an anchor bolt and a mechanical expansion anchor?

Anchor bolt is a broad term that may include cast-in anchor bolts, expansion anchors, chemical anchors and anchor rods. Mechanical expansion anchors like wedge anchors and sleeve anchors achieve retention through expansion against the base material. Different products within the anchor bolt category are not interchangeable.

Does every wedge anchor have ETA or ICC-ES approval?

No. ETA or ICC-ES approval applies only to specific products with valid evaluation reports. Buyers should confirm the exact product, report number, materials, diameters and installation conditions when approval is required.

Which materials and finishes are suitable for outdoor anchor applications?

Hot-dip galvanized or stainless steel anchors are commonly discussed for outdoor or corrosion-prone environments. The final material and finish choice should be confirmed against the project specification, exposure class and applicable evaluation documentation.

Can wedge anchors and sleeve anchors be included in one mixed order?

Yes. Mixed orders can be reviewed manually when SKU data, diameters, lengths, materials, finishes, quantities, packing and inspection requirements are clear for each anchor type.

What affects the lead time for a mixed anchor order from China?

Lead time depends on material availability, finish processing, quantity, packing complexity and inspection requirements. Final lead time is confirmed after RFQ review.
