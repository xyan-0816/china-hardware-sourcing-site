Mixed Fastener RFQ Support for Importers and Distributors

This English-first content page helps importers, distributors, contractors, and industrial buyers prepare clearer fastener inquiries before manual RFQ review.

Practical RFQ Preparation

Fastener sourcing from China works better when the buyer provides clear product names, sizes, dimensions, materials, surface finishes, standards, quantities, packaging requirements, and reference documents. Drawings, samples, and photos can reduce uncertainty when product names vary between markets.

CHS treats website content as sourcing communication support, not a final specification sheet. The final confirmation should be based on drawings, standards, samples, purchase lists, or application needs reviewed by the buyer and supplier side.

Manual Review Process

Each inquiry should be checked manually before supplier communication. The review may organize product categories, identify missing specification points, and prepare follow-up questions about material, finish, grade, thread, tolerance, packaging, and quantity.

This approach is suitable for standard fasteners, buyer-specified items, and mixed lists that include several fastener categories in one inquiry.

Fastener Sourcing FAQ

Questions commonly asked before sending a China fastener RFQ.

Does CHS provide automated public quotation amounts?

No. RFQs are reviewed manually based on the information provided by the buyer.

What information helps a fastener inquiry?

Product names, sizes, materials, finishes, standards, quantities, packaging requirements, drawings, samples, or photos can help initial review.

Can buyers send a mixed fastener list?

Yes. Mixed-SKU fastener inquiries can be organized by product type, size, material, finish, and quantity.

Are specifications confirmed only from website images?

No. Product images are representative only. Final specifications should be confirmed from drawings, standards, samples, or purchase lists.

Can buyer-specified materials and finishes be reviewed?

Yes. Material and finish directions can be reviewed conservatively based on application requirements and buyer documents.

Which categories are covered?

Core categories include bolts, studs, threaded rods, nuts, washers, screws, anchors, and selected related hardware categories.
