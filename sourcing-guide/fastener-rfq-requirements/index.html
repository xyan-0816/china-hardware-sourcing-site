Fastener RFQ Requirements Guide

A complete fastener RFQ should identify the product type, applicable standard or drawing, dimensions, thread system, material, property class or grade, finish, quantity, packing and required inspection documents. Prices, MOQ, packing, documentation, and lead time are confirmed after manual RFQ review.

Complete RFQ Checklist

Each of the following information groups helps the supplier prepare an accurate quotation. Missing items may result in incorrect pricing, wrong product assumptions, or delayed responses.

1. Product Identification

  • Product type: bolt, nut, washer, screw, anchor, stud or threaded rod
  • Product standard or drawing number

2. Dimensions

  • Diameter
  • Length
  • Thread length (for partial-thread bolts and studs)
  • Head dimensions when required

3. Thread Information

  • Metric pitch (e.g. 1.5, 1.75, 2.0 mm)
  • UNC (Unified Coarse)
  • UNF (Unified Fine)
  • 8UN (8-thread series)
  • TPI (threads per inch)

4. Material and Mechanical Requirements

  • Carbon steel
  • Stainless steel
  • Alloy steel
  • Property class or grade

5. Finish and Coating

  • Zinc plated
  • Hot-dip galvanized
  • Black oxide
  • Plain (no coating)
  • Buyer-specified coating

6. Quantity and Packing

  • Quantity per SKU
  • Carton, bag, pallet or buyer-specified packing
  • Marking and label requirements

7. Quality and Documents

  • Dimensional inspection
  • Material certificate
  • Coating documentation
  • Test report
  • Sample or drawing approval when applicable

Buyer Quick Answers

Independent answers for common fastener RFQ questions.

What information is required for a fastener RFQ?
A complete fastener RFQ should identify the product type, applicable standard or drawing, dimensions, thread system, material, property class or grade, finish, quantity, packing and required inspection documents.
Should buyers provide a standard number or a drawing?
Buyers should provide either a recognized fastener standard number or a detailed drawing in the RFQ to give the supplier a clear reference for dimensions, tolerances, threading, and acceptance criteria.
How should fastener dimensions be written in an RFQ?
Fastener dimensions should be written as diameter and length in the unit system of the standard, for example M12 x 60 for metric or 1/2-13 x 3 for inch, and should match the standard or drawing.
How should metric thread pitch be specified?
Metric thread pitch should be specified in millimeters following the diameter, for example M12 x 1.75 or M12 x 1.25 when the pitch is non-coarse, and should reference the applicable standard.
How should UNC, UNF and TPI be specified?
UNC, UNF, and TPI should be specified after the inch diameter, for example 1/2-13 UNC or 1/2-20 UNF, and the buyer should confirm which thread series and class of fit the application requires.
What material information should buyers provide?
Buyers should provide the material type such as carbon steel, stainless steel, or alloy steel, and the applicable property class or grade such as Grade 8.8, Grade 10.9, Grade 5, or Grade 2.
What is the difference between property class and grade?
Property class is the metric designation for mechanical properties such as 8.8 or 10.9, while grade is the imperial designation such as Grade 5 or Grade 8. Buyers should use the system that matches the fastener standard.
How should surface finish be specified?
Surface finish should be specified clearly by its common name such as zinc plated, hot-dip galvanized, black oxide, or plain, and the buyer should confirm the applicable coating standard or thickness requirement.
Can multiple SKUs be included in one RFQ?
Multiple SKUs can be included in one RFQ. Each SKU should have its own line item with complete specifications, quantity, and packing requirements so the supplier can quote each item separately.
What packing information should an RFQ include?
An RFQ should include packing per carton, carton weight, bag or box preference, pallet configuration, and marking or label requirements to help the supplier prepare an accurate packing and shipping plan.
What inspection documents can buyers request?
Buyers can request dimensional inspection reports, material certificates, thread gauge reports, coating thickness reports, salt spray test reports, hardness reports, tensile or proof-load reports, and packing inspection reports.
What causes delays or errors in fastener quotations?
Common causes of delays or errors include incomplete product identification, missing thread pitch, unclear finish requirements, no standard or drawing reference, and requesting price before defining specifications.

FAQ

Practical answers for overseas buyers preparing fastener sourcing documents.

What information should be included in a fastener RFQ?

A complete fastener RFQ should identify the product type, applicable standard or drawing, dimensions, thread system, material, property class or grade, finish, quantity, packing and required inspection documents.

What QC documents should buyers request before shipment?

Buyers may request MTC or MTR, certificate of conformity, dimensional report, hardness report, tensile or proof-load report, thread gauge report, coating report, packing inspection report, and photos depending on the order requirements.

What is the difference between MTC and MTR?

Both MTC and MTR relate to material test documentation. Buyers should define the required document type before production because the exact wording and content depend on the supplier and project requirements.

Why does thread GO / NO-GO inspection matter?

Thread GO / NO-GO gauges help confirm whether bolts, nuts, rods, or tapped parts fit the agreed thread requirement before shipment.

When should coating thickness or salt spray reports be requested?

Coating thickness or salt spray reports should be requested when finish performance or project documents require verification, and the requirement should be agreed before production.

How should buyers confirm lot traceability?

Buyers can ask how cartons connect to inspection lots, production batches, heat numbers, and raw material certificates to confirm lot traceability.

Why should RFQ review happen before formal quotation?

Early RFQ review reduces missing specifications, unclear documents, wrong finish assumptions, and shipment packing surprises before quotation.
